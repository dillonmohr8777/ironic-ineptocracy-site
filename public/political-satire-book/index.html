The Ironic Ineptocracy · reader dossier

A Political Satire Book for Readers Who Suspect the Forms Are Winning

The Ironic Ineptocracy is a satirical thriller about ambition, power, friendship, and the cost of surviving a system that notices competence.

For readers of institutional satire

If you like political satire that treats broken systems as characters, this is the lane: bureaucracy as horror, meritocracy as theater, and public failure wrapped in respectable stationery.

Comparable reading moods

  • the bureaucratic trap logic of Catch-22
  • the civic fable pressure of Animal Farm
  • the systems anxiety of dystopian fiction
  • the dark comedy of people trying to stay human inside bad incentives

What makes this satire different

The book is not only about politics as argument. It is about politics as machinery: donor logic, institutional cowardice, elite gatekeeping, paperwork, reputation, and the quiet terror of being processed correctly.
