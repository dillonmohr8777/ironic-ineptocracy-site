Mark

A prosthetic arm, a bad attitude, and a basement built for breaking signals without breaking people.

Who is Mark?

Mark is what survival looks like after official employment curdles into private preparation. He does not arrive with polish. He arrives with locks, equipment, suspicion, and a cabin that refuses to be only a cabin.

Role in the novel

Mark turns resistance into engineering. His spoiler safe function is practical and unnerving: when the state learns to control bodies through signals, somebody has to learn how to interrupt the signal.

Key themes

  • signal interference
  • survival engineering
  • exile after service
  • practical paranoia
